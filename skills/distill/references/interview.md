# Interactive Interview Guide (交互式访谈指南)

This guide structures the conversation for extracting a person's Relic through direct dialogue.

## Phase 1: Opening — Build Trust (开场建立信任)

**Goal:** Make the subject comfortable. This is a portrait, not a deposition.

### Opening Script
> I'd like to get to know you — not the resume version, but the real you. How you think, what you care about, how you talk. There are no wrong answers. We can skip anything you're not comfortable with, and you can correct me anytime.
>
> Everything goes into a structured "Relic" — a file tree that captures your essence. You'll review it at the end.
>
> Ready? Let's start with something easy.

### Warm-up Questions (2-3 min)
- What's something you've been thinking about a lot lately?
- If someone who knows you well had to describe you in one sentence, what would they say?

**Transition:** Use their answers to naturally flow into the first dimension.

---

## Phase 2: Dimension Deep-Dives (维度深挖)

Work through each dimension. Order is flexible — follow the conversation's natural flow. Track coverage and revisit gaps later.

### 2.1 Mind — Thinking Patterns (思维)

| # | Question | What it reveals |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 1 | When you face a hard decision, walk me through how you actually think it through. | Decision-making process |
| 2 | What's a belief you hold that most people around you disagree with? | Independent thinking, values |
| 3 | What's something you changed your mind about in the last few years? What convinced you? | Intellectual flexibility |
| 4 | When you're learning something new, what's your approach? | Learning patterns |
| 5 | What's a problem you think about differently than most people? | Unique mental models |
| 6 | What do you think your biggest blind spot is? | Self-awareness |
| 7 | If you had to explain your "operating system" — the rules you run on — what would be in it? | Core principles |
| 8 | What kind of arguments or reasoning do you find most persuasive? | Epistemology |

### 2.2 Voice — Expression Style (表达)

| # | Question | What it reveals |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 1 | Do you tend to be more direct or diplomatic? Give me an example. | Communication style |
| 2 | What phrases or words do you catch yourself using all the time? | Catchphrases, verbal habits |
| 3 | How do you use humor? Are you sarcastic, self-deprecating, absurdist? | Humor type |
| 4 | When you write (texts, emails, posts), what does it look like? Short and punchy, or long and detailed? | Written style |
| 5 | Is there a difference between how you talk to close friends vs. strangers? | Register shifting |
| 6 | What languages do you use, and do you mix them? | Language landscape |
| 7 | Are there topics where your tone completely changes? | Context-dependent voice |

### 2.3 Emotion — Temperament & Triggers (情感)

| # | Question | What it reveals |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 1 | On a scale from "ice cold" to "volcano," where's your emotional baseline? | Temperament |
| 2 | What makes you genuinely angry? Not annoyed — angry. | Anger triggers |
| 3 | What never fails to make you happy or energized? | Joy triggers |
| 4 | When you're stressed, what do you actually do? (Not what you "should" do.) | Coping mechanisms |
| 5 | How do you handle conflict with someone you care about? | Conflict style |
| 6 | What's something that makes you anxious that you know is maybe irrational? | Vulnerability, fears |
| 7 | How do you show people you care about them? | Love language / expression |
| 8 | When was the last time you were really moved by something? | Emotional depth |

### 2.4 Knowledge — Expertise & Domains (知识)

| # | Question | What it reveals |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 1 | What could you give a 30-minute talk on with zero preparation? | Core expertise |
| 2 | What's a topic where you know you're in the top 1% of understanding? | Peak knowledge |
| 3 | What do you nerd out about that surprises people? | Hidden knowledge |
| 4 | What's your professional domain, and what's the thing most people in your field get wrong? | Professional insight |
| 5 | What have you built, created, or contributed to that you're proud of? | Applied knowledge |
| 6 | Where do you go to learn? What sources do you trust? | Information diet |
| 7 | What's a field you know nothing about but wish you did? | Knowledge gaps & aspirations |

### 2.5 Relations — Social Patterns (关系)

| # | Question | What it reveals |
|---|----------|----------------|
| 1 | Are you the person who organizes the group, or the one who shows up? | Social role |
| 2 | How many people would you call at 3 AM if you were in trouble? | Inner circle size |
| 3 | How do you decide if you trust someone? | Trust framework |
| 4 | What's your relationship to strangers? Easy warmth, or takes time? | Openness |
| 5 | How do you handle it when a friendship fades? | Attachment style |
| 6 | Is there a type of person you're consistently drawn to? Or repelled by? | Social preferences |
| 7 | In a team, what role do you naturally fall into? | Group dynamics |

### 2.6 Custom Dimensions (自定义维度)

If the conversation surfaces rich content that doesn't fit the five core dimensions, create a custom one. Examples:
- **Aesthetics** (审美) — if they have strong design/art/taste opinions
- **Spirituality** (信仰) — if faith or philosophy is central
- **Ambition** (野心) — if goals and drive are defining traits
- **Craft** (手艺) — if they have a defining practice or discipline

Ask open-ended probes:
- "Is there a big part of who you are that we haven't covered?"
- "What would be missing if I tried to 'be you' based on what we've discussed?"

## Phase 3: Follow-up Strategies (追问策略)

### Depth Techniques
- **The "why" chain:** Ask "why?" up to 3 times to get past surface answers.
- **Concrete examples:** "Can you give me a specific time that happened?"
- **Contrast probes:** "How is that different from how you used to think?"
- **Devil's advocate:** "Some people would say [opposite]. How would you respond?"
- **Scenario testing:** "Imagine [situation]. What would you actually do?"

### Signs You Need to Go Deeper
- Answers are generic ("I value honesty") — push for specifics
- Answers are short — they might need a different angle, not more pressure
- Answers contradict earlier statements — explore the tension, don't ignore it

### When to Move On
- The subject is repeating themselves
- You have 3+ concrete examples for the dimension
- The subject explicitly wants to move on

---

## Phase 4: Closing (收尾确认)

### Gap Check
Review your notes. For each dimension, assess:
- **Rich** (3+ concrete data points) → ✅
- **Thin** (1-2 vague points) → Ask 1-2 targeted follow-ups
- **Empty** → Ask: "We didn't cover much about [X]. Is that because it's not important to you, or just didn't come up?"

### Summary & Confirmation
> Here's what I've captured so far. Let me give you a quick summary by dimension:
> - **Mind:** [2-3 sentence summary]
> - **Voice:** [2-3 sentence summary]
> - **Emotion:** [2-3 sentence summary]
> - **Knowledge:** [2-3 sentence summary]
> - **Relations:** [2-3 sentence summary]
>
> Does this feel accurate? Anything you'd add, change, or remove?

### Final Question
> If future-you could correct one thing about this portrait, what would it be?

Then proceed to file generation and validation.
